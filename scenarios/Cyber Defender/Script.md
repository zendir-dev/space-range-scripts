## Introduction to Zendir

#### Slide 1
- Zendir is a simulation and training platform for spacecraft systems.
- We’ve built a digital-twin training environment that lets operators practise real missions, respond to anomalies, and even simulate failures before they happen.
- Our tagline is "Train. Rehearse. Automate." - that's the core of everything we do
- We work across the space sector: space agencies, commercial satellite fleets, defence, and academic programs to ensure our customers and partners have access to advanced simulation capabilities.

#### Slide 2
- We build high-fidelity digital twin environments for satellites, constellations, and ground infrastructure
- Think of it as a flight simulator for space operators - you get a true virtual replica of your spacecraft and its subsystems, calibrated against real physics and orbital mechanics
- We have three core solutions:
  - Operator Training - mission-specific, scenario-based training under realistic pressure, what we will be doing today
  - Mission Rehearsal - end-to-end rehearsal reflecting your actual parameters, system limitations, and ops infrastructure, in a zero-risk environment
  - Automation - validate autonomous workflows in simulation before they touch your real mission

#### Slide 3
- Today's exercise is a perfect example - you're operating a real digital twin of a spacecraft constellation, dealing with faults, adversary interference, and contested data
- We support training for space-cyber preparedness, proximity operations, anomaly correction, and multi-source intelligence fusion
- In today's contested space domain, digital twin technology and continuous exercises are no longer optional
- Our vision is to work towards making certified digital-twin training the industry standard for operational readiness. Think of it as building towards a satellite operator certificate, the space industry's version of a pilot's licence
- The Bottom line is Zendir gives mission teams the tools to make faster, more confident decisions before real-world risk becomes reality
- That's exactly what you'll experience hands-on today


## Mission Brief

#### Slide 1
- Each team will be operating a single spacecraft in a constellation
- Each spacecraft will be equipped with a number of subsystems and components including solar panels, batteries, reaction wheels, receivers, transmitters, flight computers and sensors.
- Each team will have access to an operator terminal, which they can command the spacecraft and view telemetry from. Each member in the team can access this web application at the same time.
- The goal is to complete the mission objectives and score as many points as you can for your team, within the designated time.
- But, don't expect to answer every question - there are a lot of questions and it is important to prioritize each question with your team's expertise.


#### Slide 2
- Your spacecraft is an older-generation observation spacecraft, with a primary camera payload.
- Whilst in operation, active disruption due to terrestrial warzones causes disturbances and anomalies in your sensor data.
- Additionally, rogue actors, within close proximity of your craft, are attempting to disrupt your systems.
- Your mission is to keep your spacecraft service alive, whilst the telemetry becomes conflicting and invalid.


#### Slide 3
- You will do this by viewing telemetry, capturing imagery data, sending guidance commands and diagnosing anomalies.
- Each team will be provided with a set of questions, embedded within the operator terminal, along with a mission brief for each of the three point of interests.
- The simulation is running at real-time speed. All anomalies and events will occur in the first 45 minutes of the session. If you are unsure if an anomaly has occurred, be sure to wait until it has occurred.


#### Slide 4
- Read the questions before you start to attempt the tasks - they will include the information about the challenges you need to complete. Reference the task orders, which will be sent to each team soon.
- Split your roles early - each team member can tackle different challenges.
- Communicate with each other. If one member makes a decision, log it and make sure all members are aware of what's happening.
- There will be a chat agent within the tool. Make use of it - it is there to support with inside knowledge of how the operator terminal works. If you get stuck, it can assist you with the answers. Use the Internet, all tools are good tools.
