##### Introduction to Zendir

* Zendir is a simulation and training platform for spacecraft systems.
* We’ve built a digital-twin training environment that lets operators practise real missions, respond to anomalies, and even simulate failures before they happen.
* Our tagline is "Train. Rehearse. Automate." — that's the core of everything we do
* We work across the space sector: space agencies, commercial satellite fleets, defence, and academic programs



NEXT SLIDE

* We build high-fidelity digital twin environments for satellites, constellations, and ground infrastructure
* Think of it as a flight simulator for space operators — you get a true virtual replica of your spacecraft and its subsystems, calibrated against real physics and orbital mechanics
* Three core solutions:

  * Operator Training — mission-specific, scenario-based training under realistic pressure
  * Mission Rehearsal — end-to-end rehearsal reflecting your actual parameters, system limitations, and ops infrastructure, in a zero-risk environment
  * Automation — validate autonomous workflows in simulation before they touch your real mission


NEXT SLIDE

##### Mission Brief

* Each team will be operating a single spacecraft in a constellation
* Each spacecraft will be equipped with a number of spacecraft subsystems and components including solar panels, batteries, reaction wheels, receivers, transmitters, flight computers and sensors.
* Each team will have access to an operator terminal, which they can command the spacecraft and view telemetry from. Each member in the team can access this web application at the same time.
* The goal is to complete the mission objectives and score as many points as you can for your team, within the designated time.
* But don't expect to answer every question - there are a lot of questions and it is important to prioritize each question with your team's expertise.



NEXT SLIDE

* Your spacecraft is a maritime observation spacecraft, with a camera for imagery
* There will be three unique points of interest that you will be tasked to keep track of; around the Red Sea, South China Sea and Venezuelan Sea.
* While you perform your mission, a rogue adversary spacecraft will be attempting to disrupt your communications.
* Your mission is to complete the objectives whilst mitigating the attacks and threats caused by bad actors and standard spacecraft faults.



NEXT SLIDE

* You will do this by viewing telemetry, capturing imagery data, sending guidance commands and diagnosing anomalies.
* Each team will be provided with a set of questions, embedded within the operator terminal, along with a mission brief for each of the three point of interests.
* The simulation will be sped up to 5 times real-time speed. This means you will get 2-3 orbits of the world during the 1 hour exercise. Each of the three points of interest can be visited at least twice - if you miss it the first time, try again the next orbit.


NEXT SLIDE

* Read the questions before you start to attempt the tasks - they will include the information about the challenges you need to complete. Reference the task orders, which will be sent to each team soon.
* Split your roles early - each team member can tackle different challenges.
* Communicate with each other. If one member makes a decision, log it and make sure all members are aware of what's happening.
* There will be a chat agent within the tool. Make use of it - it is there to support with inside knowledge of how the operator terminal works. If you get stuck, it can assist you with the answers. Use the Internet, all tools are good tools.



##### Task Order

* The first point of interest is in the Red Sea.
* There commercial traffic has been damaged due to piracy.
* Your objectives, as per the questions relating to this location, is to determine how many vessels are damaged based on their movements over time using your camera.



NEXT SLIDE

* The next point of interest is located somewhere in the South China Sea
* There have been signs of island development and ongoing expansion by an unknown entity.
* Your objective here is to identify the island by using the camera to determine where the island might be and correlating it to a GPS coordinate.



NEXT SLIDE

* The third point of interest is located off the Coast of Venezuela.
* There, illegal vessels are failing to send correct AIS data. Using your camera to identify the locations of vessels, AIS data will be provided to you in the Task Orders.
* The task order documents will be sent to your team once the exercise starts. In those documents, you will find the AIS data associated with each vessel.
* Your objective is to determine which vessels are operating in good faith and which ones are deceiving their transmitters.
